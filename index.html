Why Moon 52 Exists

Moon 52 begins with an old thought experiment / urban myth: if you fold a piece of paper again and again, you are only 52 folds away from the paper being as thick as the distance to the moon.

At first, nothing looks different. After three folds, it still fits in your wallet. After five, the progress still feels pretty insignificant.

But something is happening.

Each fold builds on the last. Momentum compounds. The paper becomes harder to fold, but folds also becomes more significant. The escalating difficulty is not because you're failing, but because you're growing, and ultimately it's a demonstration of the power of compounding effort and work.

That's what entrepreneurship feels like.

The early stages of building a business rarely come with instant results. The work is demanding, the progress can be quiet, and most people stop before the breakthrough becomes visible.

Moon 52 exists for the founders who keep going.
